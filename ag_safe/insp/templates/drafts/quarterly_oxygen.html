QUARTERLY OXYGEN / FUEL EQUIPMENT INSPECTION CHECKLIST
Regulators and Gauges Good Needs Repair N/A
Are regulator compression nipples, nuts, gauges and regulator adjusting screws in good condition?
Are filter screens in the inlet nozzles?
Follow the steps to test high pressure regulators:
  1. Test the fuel gas and oxygen regulators with an inert gas
  2. Release regulator adjusting screw counterclockwise
  3. Open the cylinder valve and verify that the high pressure gauge is operating properly
Is the high pressure regulator leaking?
Follow the steps to test low pressure regulators:
  1. Block the outlet nozzle of the regulator using a plug or hose
  2. Adjust the regulator adjusting the screw clockwise until the low- pressure indicates a normal operating pressure
  3. Use soapy water to inspect regulator and gauge connections for leaks
Is the high pressure regulator leaking?
Reopen the cylinder valve, and check the low-pressure gauge for any slight drop in pressure (needle creep) Gauge needle creep greater than 2 to 3 psi (14 to 20 kPa) indicates thatthe diaphragm is damagedIs the cylinder valve damage?
Hoses
Visually inspect each length of hose for burns, worn areas, decay, and other defectsHoses in good condition?
Follow the steps to test hoses:
  1. Pressurize each length of hose, and run it through a water vat to check for leaks
  2. Use inert gas or oil-free air for this test at a pressure sufficient to indicate leaks, typically between 15 and 80 psi (103 and 550 kPa)
  3. Any length of hose that cannot be tested in the water vat must be tested with soapy water
    4. Hoses in good condition and not leaking?
Combination Reverse-Flow Check Valves and Flame Arresters
Visually inspect each check valve/arrester to verify that the inlet nozzle is free of oxidation, burns, and other defects. Any defects?
Follow the steps to test valve seating:
  1. Visually inspect each check valve/arrester by using reverse-flow pressure in the outlet nozzle to verify that the check valve is sealing
  2. Either blow through the unit or use an inert gas or oil-free air at a pressure between 1 and 10 psi (7 and 70 kPa), sufficient to close the check valve
Are check valves seating?
Cutting torches and mixing chambers
O-rings on the torch in good condition?
The seating surface in the mixing chamber in good condition?
The cutting tip seating surface in the torch head in good condition?
Test the complete system and follow the instructions for setting up the system, lighting the torch, and re-lighting the torch. Any defectives?
